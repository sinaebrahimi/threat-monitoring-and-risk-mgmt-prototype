                        Technical Assessment Task



Title
Design an AI-Driven OSINT Threat Monitoring and Risk Assessment Prototype


Background
The KTP project aims to develop an AI-based intelligence system to automate
open-source intelligence (OSINT) collection, analysis, and risk assessment to
support proactive threat monitoring.
As part of the KTP Associate role, the successful candidate will be expected to
design scalable and intelligent solutions that combine:
  • AI / machine learning
  • Cyber security knowledge
  • Structured and unstructured data analysis


Task overview
You are asked to design a prototype solution for an AI-driven OSINT threat
monitoring system.
Your solution should demonstrate how open-source data can be:
  • Collected
  • Processed
  • Analysed
  • Used to generate actionable threat intelligence
The project does not need to be production-ready. The focus is on your technical
reasoning, problem-solving approach, and ability to communicate your design
decisions clearly.

Specific project requirements

1. System design
Propose an architecture for the system (diagram encouraged), including:
  • Data sources
  • Data ingestion methods
  • Storage and processing pipeline

2. AI / Analytical approach
Describe methods used to:
  • Detect relevant threats
  • Extract key entities or signals
  • Assess threat severity or risk levels

3. Prototype / Demonstration (Lightweight)
Provide a small-scale implementation or pseudo-code, workflow, or sample
outputs. This is flexible and subject to your understanding and capability.
Examples may include:
  • A simple script
  • Pipeline design
  • Sample outputs
  • Notebook or prototype

4. Risk assessment framework
Propose a method to:
  • Prioritise threats

Project deliverables

1. Written report
Please prepare a report of a maximum of 10 pages (Please do not exceed this
limit) covering:
   • System design and architecture
   • Methodology and technical approach
   • AI / analytical methods used
   • Risk assessment framework
   • Key assumptions and limitations
   • Potential future improvements

2. Presentation
During the interview, please prepare a short presentation (max 10 minutes)
summarising:
   • Your understanding of the problem
   • Proposed solution and architecture
   • Key technical decisions
   • Main findings, limitations, and future improvements
